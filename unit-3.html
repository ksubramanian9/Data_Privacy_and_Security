Access Control & Authentication

Design trustworthy identity journeys with layered authentication factors and policy-driven authorization decisions.

Overview

Unit III examines the guardrails that differentiate who can access which assets and under what conditions. Learners critique authentication models, tune assurance levels, and orchestrate authorization strategies for adaptable architectures.

Key Topics

  • Passwordless and multi-factor authentication patterns.
  • Role-Based, Attribute-Based, and Policy-Based access control models.
  • Credential lifecycle management, recovery, and revocation considerations.
  • Digital signatures, certificates, and federated identity trust models.

Implementation Milestones

  1. Create a threat model for an authentication system that defends against phishing and replay attacks.
  2. Prototype a multi-factor login flow that balances usability with assurance.
  3. Compare RBAC and ABAC policies for a case study and document recommended controls.
  4. Review certificate management processes to maintain chain-of-trust integrity.
