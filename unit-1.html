Introduction to Data Privacy and Security

Establish a holistic understanding of privacy foundations, governing frameworks, and the CIA Triad that underpins resilient information systems.

Overview

Unit I introduces the essential language and guiding principles that shape responsible data handling. Learners connect regulatory expectations with privacy-by-design mindsets and develop documentation habits that make compliance auditable.

Key Topics

  • CIA Triad and the interdependence of confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
  • Privacy governance, including global regulations and institutional policies.
  • Data mapping, lifecycle documentation, and privacy impact narratives.
  • Stakeholder roles and accountability models for safeguarding information assets.

Recommended Flow

  1. Survey the privacy landscape and key terminology used throughout the course.
  2. Develop a living data inventory that reveals how information moves through systems.
  3. Apply the CIA Triad to real scenarios, highlighting trade-offs and mitigation tactics.
  4. Summarise policy alignment and document governance checkpoints for stakeholders.

Learning Activities

  • Map a data lifecycle for a chosen application and highlight privacy touchpoints.
  • Analyse a privacy policy to evaluate coverage of confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
