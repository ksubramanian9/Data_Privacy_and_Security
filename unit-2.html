Encryption Techniques

Master the cryptographic building blocks that secure data in transit and at rest, from shared secrets to public key ecosystems.

Overview

Unit II explores the mechanisms that keep communications confidential and tamper resistant. Through labs and case studies, learners evaluate the strengths and trade-offs of symmetric, asymmetric, and hashing approaches.

Key Topics

  • Cryptographic primitives including block and stream ciphers.
  • Public key infrastructure, certificate validation, and trust chains.
  • Key management lifecycles spanning generation, rotation, and escrow.
  • Integrity controls using hashing, HMACs, and digital signatures.

Lab Progression

  1. Contrast symmetric and asymmetric encryption through worked examples.
  2. Implement AES and RSA using a preferred programming language or toolkit.
  3. Secure messages with signing, hashing, and verification workflows.
  4. Evaluate key storage patterns for distributed and multi-tenant deployments.
